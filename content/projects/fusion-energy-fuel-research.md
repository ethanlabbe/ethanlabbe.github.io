For this project, I am researched and addressed the issues of manufacturing inertial confinement fusion fuel targets. This project was created and funded under the Dean's Research Award and is supervised by Dr. Ying Tsui. The aim is to find solutions to the cost and quality control issues of the current manufacturing methods using laser light scattering and spectrum analysis to determine the quality of polystyrene fusion fuel targets. My research proposes the use of spectroscopy and light scattering patterns to detect impurities and defects in the fuel targets.
